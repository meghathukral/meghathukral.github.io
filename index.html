Megha Thukral

I am a PhD student at the School of Interactive Computing, Georgia Tech. My research focuses on generalizable representation learning for health sensing, building machine learning models for sensor data and clinical structured text that generalize across users, devices, home layouts, and clinical settings. My research tackles distribution shift in health sensing under varying levels of data and label availability: adapting wearable HAR models with limited supervision, transferring across heterogeneous smart-home environments , and learning generalizable representations with self-supervised pretraining for physiological signals and for structured clinical records. I previously interned at the Samsung Research America Digital Health Lab (Biomarkers team), and at Bloomberg AI Group. I am thankful to be advised by Prof. Thomas Ploetz, and grateful to be funded by Optum AI and NSF AI-CARING.

Projects

Every project and publication below has its own page. The list is organized into three groups: papers I have led as first or co-first author, machine learning papers I have co-authored at top venues, and a final group of other and currently in-submission work.

Lead work · first or co-first author

IMWUT '25

TDOST

A layout-agnostic approach to smart-home human activity recognition. Each sensor trigger is re-described as a short natural-language phrase, and the activity classifier operates on those text-grounded representations, letting a model trained in one home generalize to other homes with different sensor layouts.
ACM TIST '25

Cross-Domain HAR

A study of few-shot transfer learning for human activity recognition across users, devices, and datasets. The paper presents a careful protocol and a baseline recipe that holds up across the major public HAR benchmarks.
NeurIPS-W '25 · ICML '26 (in review)

Wavelet-Based PPG Foundation Models

A foundation model for photoplethysmography that performs masked reconstruction directly in the wavelet domain. By masking and reconstructing specific wavelet bands rather than time-domain patches, the model captures structure ranging from sub-second pulse morphology to multi-minute physiological modulations.
AAAI '26 · co-first

AgentSense

A pipeline in which LLM-driven agents inhabit simulated homes, follow daily routines, and trigger virtual sensors as they move around, producing diverse, fully labeled smart-home sensor data without instrumenting a single real house.
