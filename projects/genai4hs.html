Generative AI and Foundation Models for Human Sensing (GenAI4HS)

Harish Haresamudram, Chi Ian Tang, Megha Thukral, Vitor Fortes Rey, Sungho Suh, Bo Zhou, et al.
Companion of the 2025 ACM International Joint Conference on Pervasive and Ubiquitous Computing (UbiComp 2025)
UbiComp 2025 Companion
The problem. The human sensing community has spent a decade building activity-, behavior-, and physiology-aware systems on top of carefully curated signal datasets. Generative AI and foundation models have meanwhile reshaped neighbouring fields, but the bridge between the two communities is still being built. We need shared venues that make it easy for sensing researchers and generative AI researchers to talk to each other.

What it is

GenAI4HS is a workshop I co-organized at UbiComp 2025. The workshop brings researchers and practitioners together to discuss the recent trends and challenges in building foundation models for human sensing, and in integrating advances from generative AI into sensor pipelines. The program covers technical contributions on generative models and foundation models for human sensing, as well as position pieces on how the field should evolve.

Why it matters

Generative AI has changed how representations are built across modalities. For human sensing the question is not whether those advances will land, but how. A dedicated forum lets the community surface what works, what does not, and what the open problems are when foundation models meet wearable and ambient signals.
