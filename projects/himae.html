HiMAE: Hierarchical Masked Autoencoders Discover Resolution-Specific Structure in Wearable Time Series

Sang-Ah Lee, Cyrus Tanade, Hao Zhou, Juhyeon Lee, Megha Thukral, Minji Han, Rebecca Choi, Md Sazzad Hossain Khan, et al.
International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2026
Paper (OpenReview) arXiv:2510.25785
The problem. Self-supervised foundation models for wearable time series have grown larger and larger, but raw wearable signals such as photoplethysmography and accelerometry contain meaningful structure at very different temporal scales. A single-scale or monolithic encoder either smears fast detail or misses slow structure, and the resulting representations are difficult to interpret in terms of which time scale they actually rely on.

Idea

HiMAE is a lightweight self-supervised objective built around hierarchical masked autoencoders. The model is trained to reconstruct masked patches of wearable time series at multiple temporal resolutions, and the resulting hierarchy of representations preserves resolution-specific structure that downstream tasks can selectively read off.

What it gives you

The trained model competes with much larger transformer foundation models on a wide range of wearable downstream tasks despite its much smaller compute and parameter footprint. Just as importantly, the hierarchical structure doubles as an interpretability tool: by inspecting which level of the hierarchy a given downstream task most relies on, the model reveals the temporal resolution at which the task lives.

Why it matters

Wearable signals on real devices need representations that are small enough to deploy and transparent enough to debug. HiMAE shows that careful hierarchical design can deliver both, and that resolution-specific interpretability falls out naturally from the architecture.
