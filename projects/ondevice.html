Towards On-Device Foundation Models for Raw Wearable Signals

Sang-Ah Lee, Cyrus Tanade, Hao Zhou, Juhyeon Lee, Megha Thukral, Baiying Lu, Sharanya Arcot Desai
NeurIPS 2025 Workshop on Learning from Time Series for Health
Paper (OpenReview)
The problem. Foundation models for raw wearable signals are typically large transformer architectures that are difficult or impossible to run directly on the smartwatch or phone where the data is collected. Sending raw signals to the cloud is expensive, undesirable for privacy, and incompatible with truly real-time use cases. Pretraining and inference both need to fit on the edge.

Idea

We design and pretrain edge-efficient foundation models for raw wearable signals that preserve representation quality while fitting within the compute, memory, and energy budgets of consumer wearable hardware. The work covers both architectural choices that keep parameter counts low and self-supervised pretraining objectives that scale to billions of unlabeled signal segments without requiring server-class infrastructure.

Why it matters

Health-grade wearable signal modeling on-device unlocks downstream applications in real-time biomarker estimation and proactive health intervention without sending raw data off the device. Bringing pretraining itself toward the edge is the first step toward foundation models that can be specialized to populations and individuals after deployment.
