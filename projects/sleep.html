Multimodal Self-Supervised Learning for Wearable Sleep Staging Using Photoplethysmography and Accelerometer Signals

Juhyeon Lee, Sang-Ah Lee, Cyrus Tanade, Viswam Nathan, Megha Thukral, Hao Zhou, Keum San Chun, Sharanya Arcot Desai
IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing (ICASSP), 2026
ICASSP 2026 (accepted)
The problem. Wearable sleep staging from photoplethysmography and accelerometry is appealing because the sensors are ubiquitous on consumer devices, but labels are hard to come by and existing single-modal models leave performance on the table. We need representations that exploit both signals jointly, scale to very large unlabeled corpora, and can be deployed in real time on low-power hardware.

Idea

We pretrain joint photoplethysmography and accelerometer representations on 23,000 hours of unlabeled wearable data using a self-supervised objective tailored to multimodal time series. On top of these representations, we use a causal Mamba sequence model that produces sleep stage predictions in a single forward pass, making the pipeline both scalable for training and suitable for real-time inference on a wrist-worn device.

Why it matters

Sleep is one of the most clinically meaningful biomarkers a wearable can estimate, and sleep staging is the foundation for many downstream sleep-health applications. A multimodal, on-device, real-time sleep staging pipeline pushes that capability out of polysomnography labs and into everyday consumer devices.
